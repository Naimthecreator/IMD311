Sports

In practice,'sport' is characterised by its social connotation, as
well as its significant link with physical effort and performance
metrics. What we consider'sport' in one context may not
be in another; sport has numerous forms and is
continuously evolving in response to society conventions, trends,
and new directions.

Badminton

Badminton is a sport that is played on a court or on a lawn using
rackets, a net, and a shuttlecock (also known as a birdie). Players serve the shuttlecock
using their rackets, sending it over the net to the opposite player. The aim is to
utilise your rackets to prevent the shuttlecock from falling on the ground on your side
(which would allow your opponent to score a point) and to finesse your racket such
that the shuttlecock falls in-bounds on your opponent's side (which would give you a point).
The lightweight shuttlecock makes badminton a pleasant, fast-paced game that may be played in
backyards, recreational areas, and even professional competitions.

Chess

Chess is a two-player board game that simulates a fight between two kingdoms. It is
one of the world's most popular games. Millions of people play it for fun as
well as for a living. Chess is a turn-based strategy game with no secrets. As a
result, the element of chance is essentially non-existent in the game.

Basketball

Basketball is a team sport in which two teams, generally made up of five players
each, compete on a rectangular court. The goal is to get the ball through a
hoop placed high on a backboard on the other team's side of the court while
stopping the opposing team from shooting it into your hoop. It is a highly
popular sport all around the globe, and it is played with a spherical,
bouncy orange (orange-brown) ball.
